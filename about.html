About the University of Miami

Founded in 1925, the University of Miami is a private research university located in Coral Gables, Florida. It has grown into a vibrant academic community, known for its commitment to teaching, research, and service to society.

Our History

The University of Miami was established with the vision to offer quality higher education in the southeastern United States. Over the decades, the university has expanded its academic programs, research facilities, and campus infrastructure. The university’s first class had only 372 students, but today it serves more than 17,000 students from all over the world.

Our Mission

The University of Miami is committed to educating and nurturing students so they may become productive members of society. The institution emphasizes the importance of research, creativity, and innovation in shaping the future.

By providing a wide range of academic programs, the university encourages students to think critically and act responsibly in a global society. The faculty members are dedicated to fostering an environment that promotes intellectual curiosity and personal growth.

Campus Life

Life at the University of Miami extends beyond the classroom. With over 600 student organizations, a thriving arts scene, and a focus on community service, there is something for everyone.

Students can engage in leadership opportunities, cultural activities, and recreational sports. The university’s vibrant campus is designed to foster collaboration and build lasting friendships.
